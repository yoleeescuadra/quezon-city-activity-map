About the study

How to read the map

In simple terms

Red areas have stronger day-associated proxies, blue areas have stronger night-associated proxies, and gray areas have closer relative values. A balanced cell can still have low signals in both views.

What you can explore

  • Switch between relative orientation and the two proxy-strength views.
  • Click a cell to compare qualitative proxy bands.
  • Open the three reference areas to see basic plausibility checks.

Important limits

The map combines public nighttime radiance and mapped urban context. It does not observe people, population movement, working hours, customer volume, or foot traffic.

Privacy-safe view

The map excludes source analysis, formulas, weights, thresholds, raw feature counts, exact scores, and cell-level analytical inputs.
